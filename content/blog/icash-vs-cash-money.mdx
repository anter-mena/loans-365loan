**iCash vs Cash Money** is not a simple winner-and-loser comparison. Both offer high-cost, short-term borrowing, but they differ in application channels, advertised funding time, repayment choices, credit-check disclosures and provincial availability. Compare the exact agreement offered to you—and the effect on your next paycheque—before choosing either company.

*Reviewed July 20, 2026 using the lenders' official Canadian product and fee pages. Product availability and terms can change. This independent comparison is general information; neither lender reviewed or sponsored it.*

## iCash vs Cash Money: Quick Comparison

The table below records published features, not a promise that an individual application will receive them.

| Check | iCash | Cash Money |
|---|---|---|
| Main payday-loan channel | Online and app | Online, phone and branch network |
| Published loan range | $100 to $1,500 | Up to the approved amount; provincial and income limits apply |
| Published cost example | $14 per $100 in its licensed provinces | $14 per $100 on current provincial payday-loan disclosures |
| Advertised funding | e-Transfer within two minutes after signing | e-Transfer in as little as 15 minutes or direct deposit |
| Credit-check statement | One hard check on a first loan, according to iCash | Ask before consenting; the payday page does not give the same specific promise |
| Repayment | Scheduled under the agreement, commonly from the bank account | AutoPay or eligible manual payment methods, including e-Transfer |
| Physical service | Online-first | Branches plus remote channels |

The cost row can look identical while the practical fit is different. A branch may matter to someone who needs in-person help. A fully online process may matter to a borrower outside business hours. Neither convenience reduces the amount due.

Use this **iCash vs Cash Money** table as a screening step, then recheck the live provincial disclosure immediately before applying. A dated comparison cannot replace the agreement presented to an individual borrower.

## 1. Compare the Total Repayment, Not the Fee Label

Where both charge $14 per $100, a $500 advance costs $70 and creates a $570 obligation. The arithmetic is:

> $500 principal + ($14 × 5) = $570 total repayment.

That is $70 for access to $500 for a short period. FCAC's national example shows that a $300, two-week payday loan with a $42 fee corresponds to an annualized rate of about 365%. The annualized figure is useful for comparing credit, while the $342 due date is the number that must fit the immediate budget.

Provincial rules and loan structures can change the exact schedule. Alberta, for example, can require instalment treatment that differs from a single-payment Ontario example. Enter your province before relying on a calculator and save the final disclosure.

Use three columns on paper:

1. cash actually received;
2. every required payment and date;
3. money left for housing, food, utilities, medication and transportation.

If essentials do not fit after repayment, a faster transfer is not a solution. Review [payday-loan alternatives](/resources/guides/payday-loan-alternatives) before accepting the agreement.

## 2. Decide Whether Online-Only or Branch Access Matters

iCash presents an online-first application and account experience. Cash Money supports an online application and telephone channel and operates physical locations. That difference affects document help, accessibility, payment questions and complaint handling—not merely convenience.

Before choosing, test the support route without applying. Can you find a verified telephone number, mailing address and complaint process? Are service hours clear? Can you retrieve the agreement if you lose access to the app? If an accommodation is required, ask how documents and identity verification can be completed.

Do not assume a storefront makes a loan affordable, or that an online lender is unsafe. Licensing, transparent terms, security and repayment capacity are the relevant tests. Verify the legal company and the payday-lending licence for your province through the consumer-affairs office rather than trusting a logo or search advertisement.

## 3. Treat Funding Speed as an Estimate

iCash currently promotes an e-Transfer shortly after an approved agreement is signed. Cash Money says eligible borrowers may receive an e-Transfer in as little as 15 minutes, with direct deposit also available. “Within” and “as little as” describe advertised best cases. Identity review, bank processing, incomplete information and system availability can take longer.

Set the bill deadline beside the realistic funding window. If a utility must be paid in ten minutes, neither lender can guarantee that outcome. Contact the biller first; a short extension may solve the timing issue without a $70 charge on $500.

In an **iCash vs Cash Money** speed comparison, approval conditions and bank processing matter more than the smallest advertised minute count.

Never pay an insurance, processing or release deposit to speed up funding. Apply only through the independently verified lender domain. A copied website or social-media account can use the names of real lenders. If someone moves the conversation to a private messaging app or asks for gift cards, cryptocurrency, a banking password or a one-time code, stop.

## 4. Check What Each Application Does to Your Credit and Data

iCash says a first application involves one hard credit check and that later loans do not. A hard inquiry can affect a score and appears on the report lenders see. Cash Money's public payday page describes application information but does not make the same specific first-loan statement, so ask whether the exact step is a hard or soft inquiry before authorizing it.

Credit inquiries are only one privacy issue. Online applications may request identity, income, employment and bank-account information. Read the consent to learn whether the lender uses document upload, bank-data access or another verification provider; what is optional; and how to withdraw access later.

Do not submit both applications simply to “see who says yes.” That doubles the places receiving sensitive data and may add inquiries. First compare public terms, confirm provincial availability, then apply to the one whose complete structure fits. Approval itself does not prove affordability.

## 5. Stress-Test the Next Payday

The most useful **iCash vs Cash Money** test is whether the complete repayment still leaves enough for essential costs under a slightly worse income or expense scenario.

Suppose take-home pay is $1,700 and essential expenses before the following payday are:

| Essential | Amount |
|---|---:|
| Rent allocation | $850 |
| Food | $260 |
| Utilities and phone | $180 |
| Transportation | $160 |
| Medication and minimum debts | $130 |
| Total essentials | $1,580 |

Only $120 remains. A $570 payday-loan repayment cannot fit without delaying an essential or borrowing again. The application may still approve an amount based on its own rules; the household calculation gives the more important answer.

Run a second version with income arriving two days late or an essential expense increasing by $75. Do not count overdraft or another loan as income. If the only recovery plan is another payday advance, the first loan is already too large.

For an existing iCash balance, read [what happens after a missed payday-loan payment](/blog/what-happens-if-you-dont-pay-a-payday-loan-canada) and contact the lender before the due date. Do not ignore notices or authorize a supposed debt helper without verification.

## 6. Compare Repayment Control and Early-Payment Rules

Cash Money publishes AutoPay and certain manual payment options. iCash schedules repayment under its agreement and describes automatic account handling on its support pages. The controlling document is the individual agreement, not this comparison.

Ask each lender:

- What amount leaves the account on each date?
- Can I make an early payment without a charge?
- How is an extra payment applied?
- What happens if the account lacks funds?
- How do I change an authorized payment method?
- When will I receive a zero-balance confirmation?

Save the agreement outside the app and place every debit in a calendar. Cancelling a pre-authorized debit does not erase a valid debt. If a withdrawal is wrong, preserve the statement, contact the lender and bank promptly, and pursue the contract dispute separately.

## 7. Choose an Alternative When the Problem Is Not Timing

A payday loan moves future income into today; it does not increase income. It can sometimes bridge a defined delay when the repayment already fits. It is poorly matched to recurring groceries, rent or minimum debt payments.

Before either lender, compare:

- a written bill extension or due-date change;
- an employer salary advance;
- a bank or credit-union overdraft or line of credit;
- a credit-card cash advance calculated in total dollars;
- selling a non-essential item;
- government benefits or local emergency assistance;
- a reputable non-profit credit counsellor when several debts no longer fit.

Record the cash available, timing, total cost, credit inquiry, data access and effect on the next paycheque for every available option. An alternative is only better when it is genuinely accessible and costs less in the reader's circumstances.

## Final iCash or Cash Money Decision

Choose neither brand on advertising speed alone. iCash may suit someone who wants an online-first process and understands its stated first-loan hard check. Cash Money may suit someone who values branch or telephone access and its published repayment channels. These are service differences, not endorsements.

For the final **iCash vs Cash Money** decision, compare the provincial agreement line by line and borrow only if the total repayment fits after every essential. If it does not, contact the biller and investigate a lower-cost option before moving future income forward.
